Freddie Freeman

Team: Atlanta Braves

Biography:


Frederick Charles Freeman is an American-Canadian professional baseball first baseman and third baseman for the Atlanta Braves of Major League Baseball (MLB).
He has been with the Braves organization since he was drafted by them out of high school in 2007. He made his MLB debut in 2010. He is a two-time MLB All-Star.
Freeman represented Canada at the 2017 World Baseball Classic, something he had always wanted to do. He was the starting first basemen on Opening Day against
the New York Mets, going 3-4, including a triple. In the first game at SunTrust Park, Freeman doubled in a couple runs. He continued to tear up Padres pitching,
hitting three homers in the series. On April 19 against the Washington Nationals, he set a franchise record for most consecutive plate appearances to get on
base with a solo homer.
